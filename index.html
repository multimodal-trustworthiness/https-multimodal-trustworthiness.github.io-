Introducing Machine-Learning-Based Data Fusion Methods for Analyzing Multimodal Data:
An Application of Measuring Trustworthiness of Microenterprises
ABSTRACT

Multimodal data, encompassing interdependent unstructured text, image, and audio data that collectively characterize the same source (with video being a prominent example), present a wealth of information for strategy researchers. We emphasize the theoretical importance of capturing the interdependence between different modalities when evaluating multimodal data. To automate the analysis of video data, we introduce cutting-edge deep machine learning and data fusion methods that holistically account for all intra- and inter-modality interdependencies. Through an empirical demonstration focused on measuring the trustworthiness of grassroot sellers in live streaming commerce, we underscore the importance of interpersonal interactions for the business success of microenterprises. We provide access to our data and algorithms to facilitate data fusion in strategy research that rely on multimodal data.
